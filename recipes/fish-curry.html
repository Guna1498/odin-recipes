Indian Fish Curry

Description

Indian Fish Curry is a flavorful coastal delicacy made by simmering tender pieces of fish in a spicy, tangy gravy rich with coconut milk, tamarind, and aromatic spices. This dish is enjoyed across India in various regional styles — from Kerala’s coconut-based curry to Goa’s tangy red curry and Bengal’s mustard-infused version.

Ingredients

  • 500g firm white fish (such as kingfish, tilapia, or cod), cut into medium pieces
  • 2 tbsp oil or coconut oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 cup coconut milk (optional for coastal style)
  • 1 small ball of tamarind soaked in water (for tanginess)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Steps

  1. Clean and wash the fish pieces thoroughly. Marinate them with salt, turmeric, and chili powder for 15–20 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a deep pan and sauté chopped onions until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Stir in chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and the oil separates.
  5. Add turmeric, chili powder, coriander, cumin, and garam masala. Mix well and cook for 2–3 minutes.
  6. Pour in tamarind water (or a few drops of lemon juice) and a little water to make the curry base.
  7. Add coconut milk if desired and bring to a gentle simmer.
  8. Carefully add the marinated fish pieces. Cover and cook on low flame for 10–12 minutes until the fish is tender and cooked through.
  9. Adjust seasoning and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  10. Serve hot with steamed rice or chapati.
